Seals are marine mammals that belong to the pinniped family. They are well adapted to life in the water, with streamlined bodies and strong flippers. Seals can be found in both Arctic and Antarctic regions, as well as temperate oceans around the world. Their thick layer of blubber keeps them warm in cold waters. Many species of seals are social animals, living in large colonies. However, some, like the leopard seal, prefer solitary life. Seals primarily feed on fish and squid, using their sharp teeth to catch prey.
